Archimedes was an ancient male Human Greek mathematician and physicist who lived during Earth's 3rd century BCE.
He was known for his work on the principle involved in using the lever, and famously said "Give me a lever and a place to stand,, and I can move the world". (PRO: "Last Flight of the Protostar, Part II")
At least two spaceships – one in the 23rd century and another in the 24th century – were named after him, as was a 23rd-century shuttle. (DIS: "The Brightest Star"; LD: "First First Contact"; SNW: "Terrarium")
