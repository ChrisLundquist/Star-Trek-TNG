The Cubes were a species of cube-shaped photonic lifeforms made of pure energy. They came into prolonged conflict with the Orbs, another photonic species, following the collision of their home nebulas. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")


== Physiology ==
Cubes were composed entirely of coherent photonic lattice structures, forming hard-light bodies of perfect cubic geometry. Their surfaces glowed with shifting grid-patterns that conveyed emotional states and communication. When destabilized or injured, Cubes left behind cubic photonic residue on nearby walls and surfaces. They were sensitive to electromagnetic surges, which could distort their forms, and were affected by tractor beams tuned to photonic signatures. Cubes could replicate through energy fission, splitting their lattice into smaller, stable offspring. In rare cases, they were capable of cross-breeding with Orbs, producing hybrids such as the child SquAaron. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")


== History ==
For over a century, the Cubes waged war against the Orbs after the Kandrix and Veraflex nebulas merged, forcing both species into overlapping territory. The conflict, often called the "Shape war", was fought as much over ideology and cultural differences as territory.
During the 24th century, the Cubes were represented by delegate Sexagus at peace talks hosted aboard the USS Cerritos. The disappearance of Sexagus' child, Quadralon, nearly derailed negotiations, leading to armed conflict between the Orbs and Cubes on the starship. The crisis ended when Quadralon and the Orb Radiara revealed they had eloped and produced a hybrid child, SquAaron. Moved by this revelation, both sides agreed to resume diplomacy, drafting what became known as the Treaty of SquAaron. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")


== Society and culture ==
Cubes valued rigidity, structure, and straight lines, considering “angles” to be the ultimate truth. This contrasted sharply with the Orbs' philosophy, which rejected edges and celebrated curves. Cube families were tightly bonded, with parents advocating strongly for their offspring. Insults toward Orbs were common, with Cubes dismissing them as “hollow” or “meaningless.” In demonstrations of power, Cubes merged into a singular construct known as the Omegacube. Despite possessing the ability to form diverse shapes, they preferred to maintain their cubic geometry, favoring stability and uniformity. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")


== Government ==
The Cubes were represented by senior delegates, often parents of prominent Cube offspring. Leadership appeared to be collective and familial, based on seniority and the ability to command respect during negotiations. Diplomatic proceedings with the Cubes were notoriously difficult, as they interpreted even minor design elements such as rounded Federation starship corridors as cultural insults. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")


== Technology ==
As photonic entities, Cubes did not rely on conventional warp vessels. Instead, they manipulated energy fields and merged into massive photonic constructs capable of traversing nebular space. Starfleet observed that Cube constructs could combine into enlarged forms like the Omegacube, though their creativity in such displays was limited. They consumed energy sources such as directed power cells and dilithium radiation to sustain themselves. (LD: "Of Gods and Angles")
