The Enolians were a humanoid species from the Alpha or Beta Quadrant.


== Physiology ==
Enolians were bipedal humanoids with a similar physiological structure to Humans. They possess two arms and stand at varying heights. Their facial features were humanoid, with eyes, a nose, and a mouth. The most notable characteristic of Enolians was the presence of ridges on their cheeks, which varied in prominence among individuals. These ridges were unique to the Enolian species and serve as a recognizable feature. (ENT: "Canamar")


== History ==
The history of the Enolian species was deeply rooted in their development as a trading civilization. Their homeworld became a hub for interstellar trade due to its strategic location and abundance of resources. The Enolians established trading posts on various planets such as Keto-Enol and Tamaal. They actively engaged in commerce with other species throughout the sector. 
The Enolians encountered the Earth ship Enterprise NX-01 captained by Jonathan Archer. It was a successful first contact however, further investigation revealed that Archer and Commander Trip Tucker had been arrested by Enolian guards. Sub-Commander T'Pol ordered the Enterprise to set a course for Keto-Enol. Archer and Trip found themselves shackled on a ship, where they learned that they have been accused of smuggling and were being taken to the penal colony of Canamar.
They encountered fellow prisoners, including Kuroda Lor-ehn and Zoumas. The prisoners engaged in confrontations with the guards. Archer took on a smuggler role to deceive the guards, while Lor-ehn formed an unexpected bond with him. They reached a rendezvous point, Tamaal, where Archer was instructed to put the ship in a decaying orbit to simulate a crash landing. The Enterprise located the transport ship, and a team boarded it to rescue Archer and Trip. They managed to transfer the prisoners and Enolian crew to a shuttle, but Kuroda refused to go, preferring death over returning to Canamar. Archer was left with a sense of injustice and questioned how many innocent people might be imprisoned there.
The Enolian official apologized to Archer for the mistake, but Archer's bitterness remained. He pondered the flawed Enolian penal system and wondered how many others might have been wrongly incarcerated on Canamar, leaving the official at a loss for words. (ENT: "Canamar")


== Society and culture ==
Enolian society was structured around commerce and law enforcement. They had developed a strong sense of order and prioritize maintaining a stable and secure environment for trade. Enolians valued obedience to the law and had strict penalties for those who broke it. Their society had a hierarchical structure, with individuals holding various positions of authority.
Enolian culture emphasized discipline, order, and efficiency. They had a deep respect for rules and regulations and expected others to adhere to them as well. Due to their involvement in law enforcement, Enolians were often viewed as authoritative figures and were known for their no-nonsense approach to maintaining order to the Enolian species and served as a recognizable feature. (ENT: "Canamar")


== Government ==
By the mid-22nd century, they were a significant interstellar power, operating hundreds of starships and several busy trading outposts, with Keto-Enol as one of the largest.
The Enolian government was structured around a central authority that oversaw law enforcement and the administration of justice. They have a well-developed judicial system that was known for its efficiency in apprehending and punishing criminals. However, there had been instances where the system had been criticized for its lack of due process and harsh treatment of prisoners. They ran the Canamar prison colony in which they incarcerated all their criminals. 
They arrested Jonathan Archer and Charles Tucker III of Earth for smuggling without any kind of proof, and the outcomes of their "trials" were predetermined. (ENT: "Canamar")


== Technology ==
Technologically, the Enolians had achieved a level of advancement comparable to many other spacefaring civilizations. They possessed warp-capable vessels for interstellar travel, although they primarily used them for trade rather than exploration. Enolian technology was characterized by its practicality and focus on enhancing law enforcement capabilities. They had developed advanced surveillance systems, energy weapons, and containment facilities for handling criminals. (ENT: "Canamar")
